Project Description

SimuCrisis is an educational simulation application developed with Unity. The purpose of SimuCrisis is to demonstrate pandemic disaster management. It models virus spread and response strategies within a virtual population using historical data. Users can take actions such as lockdowns, vaccinations, and healthcare investments to mitigate the consequences of the pandemic.

The application shows how different prevention strategies affect infection rates, recovery results, and resource usage using interactive visual analytics, such as charts, heatmaps, and reports. The Unity interface offers real-time feedback and visualization, while the backend maintains simulation results and historical datasets.

SimuCrisis offers a safe environment to explore decision-making about pandemic crises. It primarily targets educators, researchers, and policymakers. By combining data-driven modeling with visualization, SimuCrisis enhances understanding of pandemic dynamics and supports educational and institutional readiness.
